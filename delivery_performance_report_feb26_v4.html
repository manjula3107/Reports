Strategic Insights for Leadership

✅ Delivery Machine is Firing on All Cylinders

With SOW Fulfillment at 97.9% and CDO OnTime at 99.5%, delivery execution is best-in-class. The near-zero escalation posture (2 total, none new) demonstrates robust project governance. This benchmark should be preserved and scaled as the portfolio grows.

✅ Engineering Excellence is a Competitive Differentiator

The EE Score of 92% combined with Technical Assessments at 96.3% and InSprint Automation at 86.9% signals a maturing engineering culture — above industry norm, positioning Cognizant favorably in renewal and expansion discussions.

📊 Customer Sentiment is Positive but Coverage Must Scale

PCSAT at 4.68/5 and NPS at 74.5% reflect healthy relationships. However, PCSAT coverage is only 12.3%. Leadership should prioritize expanding the survey footprint to ensure score representativeness across the full portfolio.

📈 Quality Scores Solid but Assessment Gaps Are a Latent Risk

PSQ and SQS both sit at 85%+ green. However, SQS coverage is 50.9% and PBQ coverage at 63.8% — nearly half the portfolio is unassessed. Without coverage expansion, published scores may not reflect true portfolio health.

⚠️ Agile Maturity Requires Accelerated Investment

At 66.7%, Agile Maturity is the weakest engineering metric. With project coverage at 54.1%, nearly half of active projects aren't yet measured. A targeted coaching and tooling intervention is recommended to reach 75% by Q2 FY26.

⚠️ Audit Closure Below Target — 211 Overdue Items Demand Action

With 89.2% closure against a 95% target, there is a 5.8pp gap representing 211 overdue items. A structured aging analysis with BU-level accountability and a Q1 closure sprint is recommended immediately.

🚨 267 Penalty Obligations Unmet — Financial Exposure

This is the most critical risk. 267 penalty obligations not met (plus 318 non-penalty) represent financial exposure and potential SLA breach consequences. Immediate escalation to account and delivery leadership with a 30-day resolution plan is required.

🎯 Composite Score: Headroom to Close the Gap to 82%

Composite at 78% leaves meaningful headroom. Codenizant at 78.0% and QRST at 85.2% suggest selective improvement will have outsized impact. Prioritizing the bottom-quartile BUs on Codenizant adoption could push composite above the 82% threshold by Q2.
